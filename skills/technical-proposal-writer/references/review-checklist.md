# Review Checklist

Use this before final delivery or when reviewing an existing technical proposal.

## Compliance

- Every mandatory technical clause has a mapped response.
- Every scoring item has a strong, easy-to-find section.
- Tender-required catalog order, headings, file names, page limits, and format constraints are followed.
- Technical deviations and clarification items are explicit.
- Commercial/legal clauses are not rewritten as technical promises unless confirmed.

## Evidence

- Claims about certifications, cases, partnerships, team members, patents, awards, and product capabilities are backed by supplied material.
- Case studies include project name, scope, similarity, result, and proof when available.
- Screenshots, diagrams, tables, and attachments are referenced consistently.
- Placeholders are removed or listed as open questions before final delivery.

## Technical Quality

- The solution addresses the buyer's actual scenario rather than generic product marketing.
- Architecture, implementation, testing, acceptance, training, and service plans agree with each other.
- Delivery schedule is realistic and aligned with tender dates.
- Security, data, integration, performance, and migration risks are addressed when relevant.
- SLA, warranty, and on-site staffing commitments match confirmed source material.

## Language

- Prefer concrete commitments tied to evidence.
- Avoid exaggerated claims, empty adjectives, and unsupported "industry-leading" language.
- Use consistent names for the buyer, project, systems, modules, and deliverables.
- Keep paragraphs traceable to matrix rows when possible.

## Handoff Items

List items that should go to commercial/legal/project leadership:

- Price, discount, payment, taxes, invoice, and bond questions
- Legal liability, penalties, warranty interpretation, and contract deviation
- Qualifications, originals, stamps, signatures, and authorization documents
- Delivery date or staffing commitments that exceed confirmed capacity
